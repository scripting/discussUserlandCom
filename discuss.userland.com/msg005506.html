Marc, it's possible to just release a simple sample app.

And trust the universe, leadership counts for something, maybe a lot. If we show the browser vendors how to connect to servers at a higher level, the world will start to reshape around your vision.

Remember, in today's economy, it's possible to sell out for hundreds of millions of dollars just because the CEO of a super-rich company "fell in love with the management team."

When we release myUserLand.root we'll be putting unique value out there that you can't get anywhere else. There's a good chance that we're shooting ourselves in the foot by doing this.

Further, you asked for and received help from the people in this community to get that code working, it's only fair that you share the result.

A simple sample app that communicates with one of our XML-RPC servers would do the trick. Maybe a thousand flowers would bloom. And a thousand people would understand what Broadband Mechanics is doing. That couldn't hurt?
