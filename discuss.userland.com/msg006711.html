It really is very cool. I happily use a Squeak Wiki (called a Swiki) every day as my PIM and find it very very handy. My daytimer was always getting scratched out as I changed plans, etc.

You don't have to know html, though the HTML it generates on its own is pretty basic. You start a new page by editing an exiting page and putting the link in '*' like *my new page*. Then when you save that page you have a new link that when clicked opens up for editing your new page.

More info at:

http://www.squeak.org (Squeak) http://minnow.cc.gatech.edu/squeak.1 (Squeak Swiki)

Oh yea, Squeak is bit for bit compatible on a load of systems, Macs, Win32, Unixes of various sorts, WinCE, etc and any of these systems can run a Swiki with networking.
