My fiance, Josie, woke up yesterday morning from a dream where she
realized she should check out the vans.com site (she's like that).
There she found a product in their catalog with a purchase price of
$0.00.  She promptly placed an order for a pair and told a number of
friends, who also placed orders.  The Vans company caught the mistake
(probably during fulfillment), claiming Y2K issues were involved.  In
a very humorous customer-service email enclosed below, they chose to
award her a free pair of shoes, although they denied all the friend's
orders.  The email trail is below.


Feel free to use this anecdote if you need a Y2K/E-commerce story for
a client :)


-- begin order confirmation --


Date: Tue, 4 Jan 2000 09:45:29 -0800
From: custserv@VANSSHOES.COM
To: otter@*


Dear Ms. kirkland,  


  
Thank you for shopping at Vans.com. 


Your order has been accepted and and your payment information has been
verified.  You can check on your order status at http://shop.vans.com.
You will receive a confirmation email when your order has been
shipped.


Your order reference number is 9091. 


Item:                                            Qty:    Unit Price:  


OLD SKOOL'S (Red/red cyber print , Size 8.5)     1             $.00    
 


Item Total:       $.00  
Sales Tax:        $.00  
Shipping:        $6.95 


Total:           $6.95


-- end --


-- begin "busted" email --


Dear Josie: Congratulations on your alertness in discovering our Y2K
glitch, and thanks for your alacrity and marketing acumen in notifying
your friends Heather, Gina, Joe, Philip, Elizabeth, and Allison in a
generous attempt to spread your good fortune.  For your efforts we
have decided to send you the Red/red cyber print Old Skools that you
ordered, for just the shipping fee of $6.95 that was charged to your
card.  However, please convey our condolences to your six Bay-area
friends, as they will have to go back online and re-order (and pay the
current, corrected price) for their shoes in order for their orders to
be processed.  Happy Millennium!


Richard Mooney, shop.vans guy.
