Dave's comments on Visicalc bring back old memories.

I started using Visicalc on my Apple II+ (48k) in 1982. Later when I worked for Omega Microware, Inc., I helped sell "SuperExpander"; a product that used a 128K board to give Visical more than the base 256 colums and rows.

Later in 1983 I taught Visicalc at Triton College from 1983-1985.

In some ways I owe my computer carreer to Visicalc and SuperExpander. But that is another story...
