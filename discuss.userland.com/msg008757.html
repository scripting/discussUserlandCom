I'm going to explore this, try to buy a license to the feeds that the HTML versions of the quote services use, and see what walls I hit, if any.

I've looked into this before. I don't have all the URLs immediately available, but here's what I remember:

  • Day-old data is available for $20/month from a number of sources. These sources seem to be small, obscure companies for the most part.
  • 15-minute-old data is available, but costs real $$$. The pricing model is usually calculated on a per-end-user basis, and you generally need to know how many people could see the data.
  • Up-to-the-minute data is available, but it's very, very expensive.

The market data is owned by the various exchanges. They have lots of rules about how it can be used.
