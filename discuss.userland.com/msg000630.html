I use my browser to read the daily news, both from commercial newspapers and sites like Scripting News. I want speed without getting a 56k modem. I don't need images.

I want a browser which can operate in a "stripped-down" mode, or perhaps many stripped down modes, so that I can progressively discard bells and whistles until I get to a comfortable response time.

WannaBe (first referenced in Scripting News) is stripped down and works fast. For example, the Mac Resource Page today was ready to view in 5 seconds with WannaBe. MS Explorer took 18 seconds. It could use some more features, but it demonstrates (a) the possibilities, and (b) the terrible performance of Netscape and Explorer.
