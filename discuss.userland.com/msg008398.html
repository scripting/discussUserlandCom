I suggest that the IETF is also worth considering as a standards body. Its primary benefit over the W3C is that its membership is completely open; there's no "joining" as such, just participation on mailing lists and at meetings. It's even free (except the meetings, since there are no dues to defray their costs).

For more details on the Internet Standards Process that the IETF uses, see RFC 2026.
