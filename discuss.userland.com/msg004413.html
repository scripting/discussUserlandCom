Here's an interesting news article on the failure of self policing privacy issues on the web --

Truste clears Microsoft on technicality http://www.news.com/News/Item/0,4,34114,00.html?st.ne.lh..ni

Many commercial web sites have been using the Truste seal of approval to assuage privacy concerns of their readers. This article suggests that Truste has been doing as good a job as can be expected, but is inherently inadequate for the role of policing privacy in the internet age.
