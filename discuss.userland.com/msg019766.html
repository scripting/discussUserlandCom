A more direct statistical argument:

P( a or not a ) = 1
P( you or not you ) = 1
p( you ) = 1- P( not you )

So the real question is, do you exist?

or

If we put you in a sealed box and didn't look, would you half exist and half not exist? At the same time?

Or perhaps you want to argue from the line of:

What is the probabability that you exist given the number of possible other existences. (it's something like infinity/(infinity^infinity), (or replace infinity with something on the order of 10^80 and probably more confusingly phrased as 'what is the probability of the universe's current quantum state'.) Which can be rounded off to a very small number. )

So based on faulty statistics, hand waving quantum mechanics, and round off error, we can conclude that the probablilty that you exist in your current state is ... 0.
