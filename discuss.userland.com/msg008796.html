There is a problem with Frontiers automatic linking mechanism - the one that automaticly links a quoted phrase to a page with the same title, if there is one.

When the site grovs - and DG & mainResponder makes it easy to spread responsibility for making new pages - a rising number of freak links occur.

Like on http://docserver.userland.com/tcp/sendMail where the phrase "Let's Have Fun!" in Examples tcp.sendMail ("dave@userland.com", "Hello!", "Let's Have Fun!") has becomed automaticly linked.

I know there is at least two solutions for this.

  • Escaping the quotes by putting a backslash before them. But if the 'linked to'-page is made after my page, the risk is big that it will be there for quite some time before I discover the freak link. Also, I find it a bit 'Microsoft Word' to be forced to take extra messures so that the automatic helper will not mess up my text.

  • There is a #directive to shut it of. But I like the ease of it, I would just wish the mechanism to be triggered by something a bit less common than just quoting a phrase.
