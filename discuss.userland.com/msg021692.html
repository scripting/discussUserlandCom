Another interesting thing that's happening is the way that the number of words that are commonly used (at least in America) is shrinking. Josh, I can only answer these two parts this post...my original statement referred kinda clinically to how folks acquire language...(yikes the education background rears its malformed head)...which is by virtue of context and experience with language. Context in the sense that you discover the meanings of words typically by concluding based on the words surrounding it...I know a guy who pronounces savvy (sah vee) rather than sav vee---my guess is that he has read it and not been able to notice its use in speech or make the connection. Whoa boy....This same guy has a ton of words he misuses like that....

No big deal really but, the appearance to those who know those words is that something is amiss. So, (sorry for the delay) little kids acquire language by being surrounded by it. They may not know what a word means but, by using the context that it was used correctly by another speaker they come to get a "feel" for it.
