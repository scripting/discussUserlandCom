Digging up background for the tours for the Frontier site, I came across this specification, written in 1960, that describes what was then a new programming language called Algol 60. It's the basic design for many current programming languages, including C, Pascal, Java, JavaScript, and UserTalk, the language built into Frontier. I was five years old when this spec came out.

Except for the GOTO statement and keywords appearing in 'single quotes', the design of Algol-60 is the way all block structured languages work today. Was it the first language to be specified by a BNF grammar? I'm not sure. Lisp may have been first with a formal definition. Most earlier languages were designed in an ad hoc way, and could not be formally specified.
